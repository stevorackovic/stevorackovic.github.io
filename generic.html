Curriculum Vitae

Here you can find some details of my career that (for some reason) I find important.

ECMI 2021 Students Competition

We Won!

My two colleagues, Filipa Valdeira (University of Milan) and Greta Malaspina (University of Novi Sad), and I entered the student competition organized by ECMI. The goal was to give a mathematical model that would fit the data of COVID-19 in Great Britain and Israel - these are the two countries that initially had the highest percentage of the vaccinated population. And guess what!? We won!

We will present our solution at the Educational Committee of ECMI next spring, and now we are invited to make a full paper for publication - with more detailed experiments and final conclusions.

EUSIPCO 2021

In August 2021, I had a chance to present our paper "Clustering of the Blendshape Facial Model" at the EUSIPCO. This was so exciting for me as this was my first publication, and it was such a great conference! For now, you can find the paper (and the scripts) in my github repository.

EURO 2021

In July 2021, I was invited to present my research results at Εuro 2021, the largest and most important conference for Operational Research and Management Science (OR/MS) in Europe organized by EURO – the European Association of Operational Research Society and the Hellenic Operational Research Society (HELORS). It was great to go to a conference in person again! Not to mention that Greece is always nice.

AAAS Student E-poster Competition

Third Place!

In January 2021, me and my two colleagues from the University of Milan, Filipa Valdeira and Rongjiao Ji, entered the e-Poster competition for the graduate students within AAAS annual meeting 'Understanding Dynamic Ecosystems'. We entered with a poster titled 'Meet my Avatar' that merges the ideas from the three research fields: inverse rig estimation, face reconstruction, and emotion recognition. This yields a pipeline that would enhance avatar-based communication over social networks. Our poster won third place, and it will appear in the proceedings of the Science journal.

ECMI Postgraduate / VI Iberian / Modelling Week

Automatic optimization of line roads

In July 2019, together with other BIGMATH colleagues and many other students from all around Europe, I entered a graduate student's competition co-organized by the European Consortium for Mathematics and Industry (ECMI), the Spanish Network for Mathematics-Industry (math-in), the Portuguese Network of Mathematics for Industry and Innovation (PT-MATHS-IN), and the H2020-MSCA-RISE NeEDS project (NeEDS). The challenge assigned to my group concerned finding the optimal route for building the road --- the terrain was mountainy; hence there were many possibilities for making roundabouts, bridges, or tunnels, and each choice comes at a price. We had a great time modeling the solution.

Entering PhD in Statistics and Stochastic Processes

In March 2019, I became a Ph.D. candidate at Instituto Superior Tecnico, Lisbon. The program is within the Marie Curie industrial doctorates BIGMATH --- Big Data Challenges for Mathematics. The principal areas of interest are optimization, statistics, and large-scale linear algebra for Big Data. More specifically, my research is concerned with optimizing the algorithms used for a 3D character face animation to produce more accurate results in real-time. The project is an interdisciplinary cooperation between academia (Instituto Superior Tecnico, Lisbon and Faculty of Sciences, Novi Sad) and industry (3Laterl animation studio, Epic Games). The curriculum covers ML, optimization, and statistics with a high demand for both theoretical and practical skills.

MSc in Applied Mathematics - Data Science

In September 2018, I earned an MSc degree in Applied Mathematics - Data Science at the Faculty of Sciences UNS. This two-year master's program focused on extracting knowledge from data, utilizing ML, DL, optimization, and signal processing tools. My master thesis, titled "Parallel Implementation of Machine Learning Algorithms using PyCOMPSs," concerns the software developed by the I-BiDaaS project and was conducted under the supervision of professor Dušan Jakovetić.

Junior Researcher at Faculty of Sciences UNS

From April 2018, I started working as a Junior Researcher at the Faculty of Sciences UNS, within an international project I-BiDaaS --- Industrial-Driven Big Data as a Self-Service Solution. My role is model for distributed implementation of the standard Machine Learning algorithms.

ECMI SIG Workshop

Entering MSC in Applied Mathematics - Data Science
